About

A focused platform for maritime asset opportunities.

Vaari Capital is being built to identify, structure and execute differentiated maritime investments across shipping and adjacent sectors for investors seeking commercially grounded exposure to real assets.

Platform overview

The platform is intended to pursue select maritime opportunities where sector understanding, transaction discipline and structuring capability can support attractive risk-adjusted outcomes.

  • Maritime-led perspective across shipping and related real asset strategies
  • Emphasis on commercial visibility, counterparties and downside discipline
  • Execution-oriented approach rooted in structuring and transaction judgement
  • Flexible mandate across asset classes and investor return profiles
Direct investing approach Focused on opportunities where underwriting discipline and market knowledge matter.
Real asset orientation Maritime investments grounded in asset relevance, market context and structure.

What defines Vaari

Vaari Capital is intended to sit at the intersection of sector specialization, institutional presentation and practical execution. The emphasis is on selectivity rather than volume, and on opportunities where structure and judgement can materially influence outcomes.

Chris Peters

Partner

Chris is a senior maritime executive with over 35 years of experience across maritime banking, ship ownership, and investment leadership. He joined Montfort Maritime in April 2023 as SEO and Partner of Montfort Capital. Prior to that, he was the CEO of Emirates Ship Investment Company (Eships), where he also served as CFO. He held leadership roles in Eships through Eships ownership under Mubadala, Oldendorff and finally Tristar.

Prior to this, he held senior shipping banking roles with Nordea and GE, with experience across shipping IPO, ECM, and DCM transactions.

He brings deep commercial judgment, capital allocation expertise, and extensive experience across ship owning, structured finance, and maritime transactions.

Across his career, he has completed shipping asset acquisitions with a gross asset value of over USD 2 billion.

Chris holds a BSc in Financial Economics from Birkbeck College, University of London, and an MBA in Strategy and Finance from City University, London.

Ambar Sangwan

Partner

Ambar is a senior investment professional with over 22 years of global experience in maritime investments, M&A, strategic advisory, and fundraising. He joined Montfort Capital as Partner in May 2025. Prior to this, he was Chief Strategy Officer and Board member at Synergy Marine Group, where he led the acquisition of Maersk Tankers’ technical management business and the sale of a minority stake to Searchlight Capital.

He previously managed over USD 250 million of liquid investments for a family office and led corporate development at Anglo-Eastern, including playing a key role in its merger with Univan.

He brings deep origination capability, transaction execution expertise, strategic judgment, and a differentiated network across the maritime and shipping ecosystem.

Across his career, he has completed maritime corporate and asset transactions totaling over USD 1.3 billion.

Ambar is a former seafarer and holds an MBA from HEC Paris, with additional coursework at Harvard Business School and The Fletcher School of Law and Diplomacy.

Strategy

Built around asset quality, cash flow clarity and disciplined structuring.

The platform is intended to evaluate maritime opportunities where commercial fundamentals, asset backing and transaction structure can support resilient investment cases.

01

Underwrite from the asset upward

Assess maritime opportunities through a bottom-up commercial, technical and structural lens rather than through thematic positioning alone.

02

Prioritize visibility and resilience

Focus on situations where earnings durability, counterparty quality and contractual alignment can improve investment clarity.

03

Selectively pursue differentiated situations

Target opportunities where sector insight, network access or structuring capability may create a meaningful edge in origination or underwriting.
